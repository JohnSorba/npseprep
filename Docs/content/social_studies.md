
# NPSE General Paper — Social Studies (Class 6 Syllabus)

## Second Stage of Basic Education — Class 6

### Unit 1: Sierra Leone and Her Immediate Neighbours
- Locate Liberia, Guinea and Sierra Leone on a map
- Describe sizes, shapes, and origins of country names
- Measure distances between cities and towns
- Use map scales accurately

### Unit 2: Relief, Climate and Vegetation
- Define relief, climate and vegetation
- Describe physical features of Liberia, Sierra Leone and Guinea
- Explain reasons for hot and humid climate
- Draw and interpret simple climate graphs
- Compare climate and vegetation across the three countries

### Unit 3: History and National Symbols
- Identify historical commonalities among the three countries
- Describe the founding histories of Liberia and Guinea
- Compare Freetown and Monrovia
- Draw and explain flags and coats of arms of Sierra Leone, Liberia and Guinea

### Unit 4: Moving Between Countries
- Explain movement of people and goods across borders
- Discuss cross-border trade and its importance
- Identify border security institutions
- Explain the need for secure borders

### Unit 5: Population and Economic Activities
- Define population and population density
- Explain effects of excessive population growth
- Draw and interpret population pyramids
- Identify and classify economic activities
- Explain environmental impacts of economic activities

### Unit 6: Cooperation and Conflict in the Mano River Union Countries
- Define cooperation and conflict
- Explain the need for cooperation among countries
- Describe the history, achievements and challenges of the Mano River Union
- Explain causes, effects and resolution of civil wars in Liberia and Sierra Leone

### Unit 7: European Rule in West Africa
- Explain European rule and motivations
- Describe reactions of West Africans
- Analyse patterns and effects of European colonialism

### Unit 8: Sierra Leone and the West African Community of Nations
- Classify West African countries by location and language
- Identify advantages of coastal countries and disadvantages of landlocked ones
- Describe economic activities in West Africa
- Explain the formation, achievements and challenges of ECOWAS

### Unit 9: Migration and Health Issues in West Africa
- Explain causes and effects of migration
- Describe youth migration to Europe and its consequences
- Explain the spread of diseases such as Ebola and COVID-19
- Propose prevention strategies for communicable diseases
